# Cybersecurity Governance & Risk Analysis — SpaceTech Corp

A board-level cybersecurity risk assessment of a fictional satellite operator (SpaceTech Corp). Starting from a set of operational dashboards, this project identifies governance and strategic gaps, evaluates their risk impact, maps the organization against a recognized maturity model, and delivers prioritized recommendations to executive leadership.

## What's in This Project

| File | What it is |
|------|------------|
| `SpaceTech_Board_Memo.docx` | Executive memo to the Board: key findings and prioritized recommendations with named owners and timelines |
| `SpaceTech_CMMI_Maturity_Assessment.docx` | Maturity rating of each domain against the CMMI model, with evidence-based justification |
| `analysis/` | Structured analysis mapping observations to gaps, risk impacts, and recommendations |
| `dashboards/` | The five operational dashboards the analysis is built on |

## The Scenario

SpaceTech Corp's enterprise cyber risk score rose 24% over six months (62 to 77) with no board-directed corrective action, while two Key Risk Indicators remained Red and Satellite Command systems were flagged "Not Accepted" on the Board's own risk register. The analysis traces operational evidence to its governance consequences and lays out what leadership must do.

## Domains Assessed

- Governance & Leadership
- Risk Management
- Policy & Compliance
- Technology Enablement

## Approach

Each observation is traced from technical evidence through to its governance gap, organizational risk impact, and a recommendation with assigned ownership and reporting cadence. Findings are then rated against the CMMI maturity model (Level 1–5) to give leadership a defensible, framework-based picture of where the organization stands and what reaching the next level requires.

## Selected Findings

- Active exploitation pressure on Satellite Command Server 1 — 38% patched, 12 critical vulnerabilities, with an unauthorized command attempt detected minutes after a Ground Station malware event.
- Vendor access as the highest composite risk domain (~80%), with MFA coverage at only 61% and a confirmed VPN enforcement failure.
- A 47-incident backlog across all categories with detection capability in place but no automated response.
- Overall maturity rated Level 1–2 (Transitional): basic structures exist, but processes are not consistently enforced enterprise-wide.
